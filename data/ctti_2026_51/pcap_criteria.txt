ANNEX 2. CRITERIS D'ADJUDICACIÓ
Contract: CTTI-2026-51
Serveis gestionats de Centre d'Operacions de Seguretat (SOC) per a la Generalitat de Catalunya

SCORING OVERVIEW
Total maximum score: 100 points
- Criteria evaluated by judgement of value (judici de valor): maximum 49 points
- Criteria evaluated automatically (fórmules): maximum 51 points

=============================================================
SECTION 2.a — CRITERIA EVALUATED BY JUDGEMENT OF VALUE (49 points)
=============================================================

For each criterion evaluable by judgement of value, the technical evaluation
will be carried out attending to the assessable aspects indicated in each criterion.

Where an offer equals or exceeds 90% of the technical valuation of a criterion
with its own identity (CIP), the final score will be obtained by applying the
formula from Directriu 1/2020:

Pop = P * (VTop / VTmv)

Where:
- Pop = score for the criterion for the offer being evaluated
- P = maximum score for the criterion
- VTop = technical valuation of the criterion for the offer being scored
- VTmv = technical valuation of the criterion with the best valuation

CRITERION 1.1 — EQUIP I QUALIFICACIONS DEL SOC (CIP)
Maximum points: 15

Reference sections in PPT: apartats 2.1 i 5.1

What to evaluate:
The composition, certifications and experience of the dedicated SOC team will
be evaluated, attending to the number of full-time equivalent (FTE) analysts
allocated exclusively to this contract, the professional certifications held
(GIAC GCIH, GCIA, OSCP, CEH, CISSP or equivalent recognised by CCN), the
demonstrated experience in security operations for public-sector or critical
infrastructure environments, and the continuity and seniority distribution
across shift patterns to ensure genuine 24/7 operational coverage.

---

CRITERION 1.2 — CAPACITAT DE DETECCIÓ I RESPOSTA (CIP)
Maximum points: 20

Reference sections in PPT: apartats 2.2, 2.3, 5.2 i 5.3

What to evaluate:

- The SIEM and SOAR tooling proposed to support detection and response operations
  will be evaluated, attending to the maturity of the platform, the breadth of
  native integrations with CTTI's existing infrastructure (including the xarxa
  troncal, endpoint estate and cloud environments), and the platform's capacity
  to scale to the volume of events generated by the Generalitat's infrastructure.
  (maximum 7.00 points)

- The quality and coverage of the detection playbook library will be evaluated.
  Specifically: the number of documented detection use cases, the degree to which
  playbooks are pre-mapped to MITRE ATT&CK tactics and techniques relevant to
  public administration threat actors, and the proposed process for updating
  playbooks in response to emerging threats.
  (maximum 7.00 points)

- The incident response capability will be evaluated, including the structured
  IR workflow from initial triage to containment, eradication and recovery; the
  availability and qualifications of IR specialists for major incidents; and the
  proposed coordination process with the Agència de Ciberseguretat de Catalunya
  for incidents affecting critical Generalitat services.
  (maximum 6.00 points)

---

CRITERION 1.3 — INTEL·LIGÈNCIA D'AMENACES (CIP)
Maximum points: 9

Reference sections in PPT: apartats 2.4 i 5.4

What to evaluate:
The threat intelligence capability proposed for this contract will be evaluated,
attending to: the commercial and open-source threat intelligence feeds subscribed
and operationalised within the SIEM/SOAR platform; the existence of a dedicated
Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) team or function with named certified analysts;
the proposed threat hunting programme, including frequency, methodology and
deliverables; and the evidence of prior threat intelligence contributions to
public-sector or national CSIRT communities.

---

CRITERION 1.4 — REPORTING I GOVERNANÇA (CIP)
Maximum points: 5

Reference sections in PPT: apartat 5.5

What to evaluate:
The SLA reporting framework, escalation procedures and governance model will be
evaluated, attending to: the frequency and content of operational and executive
reporting deliverables; the clarity and testability of the proposed SLA definitions
and measurement methodology; the escalation path for critical incidents and
the defined notification timelines to CTTI management; and the governance
structures — including service review cadence and contract management — proposed
for the duration of the contract.
