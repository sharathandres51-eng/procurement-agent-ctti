PLEC DE PRESCRIPCIONS TÈCNIQUES PARTICULARS — RELEVANT SECTIONS
Contract: CTTI-2026-51
Serveis gestionats de Centre d'Operacions de Seguretat (SOC) per a la Generalitat de Catalunya

=============================================================
SECTION 3.1 — SOC SERVICE SCOPE AND OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================

3.1.1 Service Scope

The contractor must provide a fully managed Security Operations Centre (SOC)
service covering continuous 24 hours/day, 7 days/week, 365 days/year monitoring,
detection, analysis and response for the following in-scope environments:

- Generalitat de Catalunya xarxa troncal: all perimeter and internal network
  traffic collection points designated by CTTI.
- Endpoint estate: Windows and Linux endpoints managed by CTTI, estimated 12,000
  devices at contract start, subject to growth over the contract term.
- Cloud workloads: Azure and AWS environments operated by or on behalf of CTTI,
  including those migrated under CTTI-2026-44.
- On-premise data centres: BSC private cloud environment and any remaining
  legacy on-premise infrastructure during the transition period.
- SaaS applications: designated Microsoft 365 tenant and other approved SaaS
  platforms identified by CTTI.

3.1.2 Minimum Coverage Requirement

The service must provide guaranteed analyst coverage of at minimum 20 hours
per day, with a documented escalation path ensuring that all hours are covered
by a qualified analyst. 24/7 full analyst coverage is the target. Automated
alerting and triage tools may supplement but not replace human analyst coverage.

3.1.3 Event and Incident Classification

The contractor must implement and maintain a four-tier event classification
scheme compatible with the CCN-CERT incident severity taxonomy:

  Tier    CCN severity equivalent    Target detection time    Target response time
  P1      Crítica (5)                <= 15 minutes            <= 30 minutes
  P2      Molt alta (4)              <= 30 minutes            <= 2 hours
  P3      Alta (3)                   <= 1 hour                <= 8 hours
  P4      Mitja (2) and below        <= 4 hours               <= 24 hours

All time targets are measured from the first event indicator ingested into the
SIEM that is associated with the confirmed incident.

SECTION 3.2 — TECHNOLOGY PLATFORM REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================

3.2.1 SIEM Requirements

The SIEM platform must:
- Support ingestion of at minimum 50,000 events per second (EPS) at contract
  start, with demonstrated capacity to scale to 150,000 EPS without architectural
  change, to accommodate growth and peak event volumes.
- Retain raw log data for a minimum of 12 months online (searchable) and 36
  months in cold storage, in line with the Agència de Ciberseguretat de
  Catalunya's data retention requirements for security events.
- Provide CTTI with read-only access to the SIEM environment, including the
  ability to run ad-hoc queries against raw log data without contractor assistance.
- Integrate natively with the Agència de Ciberseguretat de Catalunya's shared
  threat intelligence platform.

3.2.2 SOAR Requirements

The SOAR platform must:
- Automate initial triage and enrichment of all P3 and P4 alerts without
  requiring manual analyst intervention for the initial enrichment step.
- Provide documented, version-controlled playbooks in a machine-readable format
  (e.g. YAML or JSON) for all automated response actions.
- Allow CTTI to review and approve changes to automated response playbooks
  that affect production CTTI systems before deployment.
- Integrate bi-directionally with CTTI's ITSM platform for automated incident
  ticket creation, assignment and status synchronisation.

3.2.3 Detection Use Case Library

The contractor must maintain and deliver a detection use case library covering:
- A minimum of 200 documented detection use cases at contract start, mapped
  to MITRE ATT&CK tactics and techniques.
- Coverage of at minimum 15 MITRE ATT&CK tactics relevant to threats targeting
  Spanish public administration environments, as defined by CCN-CERT advisories.
- A formal process for adding new use cases in response to CCN-CERT alerts,
  with a target of deploying a new or updated use case within 5 business days
  of a CCN-CERT advisory classified alta or higher.

SECTION 3.3 — THREAT INTELLIGENCE REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================

3.3.1 Threat Intelligence Feeds

The contractor must operationalise threat intelligence from at minimum:
- One commercial threat intelligence platform (e.g. Recorded Future, Mandiant
  Advantage, CrowdStrike Falcon Intelligence or equivalent).
- CCN-CERT REYES platform indicators, mandatory for public-sector contracts.
- Two curated open-source feeds (e.g. MISP community, AlienVault OTX or
  equivalent), with documented curation and validation processes.

All ingested indicators of compromise (IoCs) must be operationalised in the
SIEM within 4 hours of receipt for critical indicators.

3.3.2 Threat Hunting

The contractor must provide a proactive threat hunting service including:
- Minimum one dedicated threat hunting engagement per month, with a documented
  scope and hypothesis-driven methodology.
- Delivery of a written threat hunting report to CTTI within 5 business days
  of each engagement, covering scope, methodology, findings and recommendations.
- At minimum one annual threat hunting engagement specifically targeting
  threats to Catalan public administration based on intelligence from CCN-CERT
  and ISAC communities.

SECTION 4.1 — TEAM ROLE REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================

4.1.1 SOC Team Roles

- Responsable de compte (Account Manager): Single contractual point of contact.
  Cannot be subcontracted.

- SOC Manager: Responsible for day-to-day SOC operations management, SLA
  oversight, escalation coordination and service review preparation. Must be
  a named individual allocated to this contract with minimum 50% dedication.

- Analistes de seguretat de nivell 1 (L1 Analysts): First-line triage, alert
  validation and initial incident classification. Minimum 4 FTE dedicated to
  this contract across shift patterns.

- Analistes de seguretat de nivell 2 (L2 Analysts): Deep investigation,
  correlation analysis, containment recommendation and playbook execution.
  Minimum 2 FTE dedicated to this contract.

- Analistes de seguretat de nivell 3 / Especialistes IR (L3/IR Analysts):
  Major incident response, forensic analysis, advanced threat hunting.
  Minimum 1 FTE dedicated to this contract, with named backup coverage.

- Analista CTI (CTI Analyst): Dedicated threat intelligence analyst responsible
  for feed curation, IoC operationalisation and threat hunting programme delivery.
  Minimum 1 FTE.

4.1.2 Minimum Certification Requirements

L1 Analysts: CompTIA Security+ or CCN-STIC equivalent. Minimum 1 year SOC experience.
L2 Analysts: GIAC GCIH or GCIA, or CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker). Minimum 3 years
  SOC experience, with at least 1 year at L2 or equivalent tier.
L3/IR Analysts: GIAC GCFE, GREM, or OSCP. Minimum 5 years SOC/IR experience.
CTI Analyst: GIAC GCTI or equivalent recognised CTI certification. Minimum 3 years
  threat intelligence experience.
SOC Manager: CISSP or CISM. Minimum 7 years in information security, minimum
  3 years in SOC management or equivalent leadership role.

4.1.3 Minimum Team Composition

SECTION 5 — SERVICE DELIVERY PHASES
=============================================================

5.1 Phase 1 — Integració i posada en marxa del SOC (SOC Onboarding)
Duration: 3 months from contract start date.

Must include:
- Log source onboarding and validation for all in-scope environments
- SIEM and SOAR platform configuration and use case deployment
- Integration with CTTI ITSM and notification platforms
- Baseline establishment for anomaly detection
- Initial threat intelligence feed operationalisation
- Tabletop exercise with CTTI to validate IR workflows
- Delivery of initial detection use case library and playbook documentation

5.2 Phase 2 — Operació contínua (Continuous Operations)
Duration: Contract term following Phase 1 certification.

Minimum activities:
- 24/7 monitoring and alert triage (or per agreed coverage model)
- Monthly threat hunting engagements per Section 3.3.2
- Quarterly detection use case review and update
- Monthly operational reporting and quarterly executive reporting
- Ongoing CCN-CERT advisory integration and use case updates
- Annual major IR simulation exercise with CTTI

5.3 Pla de transició i sortida (Exit and Transition Plan)
Duration: 3 months before contract end.

Minimum requirements:
- Full export of SIEM raw log data in a CTTI-accessible format
- Documentation of all detection use cases in an open, portable format
- Knowledge transfer to CTTI or incoming contractor covering all operational
  procedures, playbooks and integration configurations
- No service degradation during the transition period

SECTION 6 — SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS (ANS) — SUMMARY
=============================================================

Key ANS indicators applicable to automatic scoring criteria (Sobre C):
- SO-COB01: Analyst coverage hours per day (target >= 20 hours)
- SO-DET01: Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) for P1 incidents in minutes
- SO-RES01: Mean Time to Respond (MTTR) for P1 incidents in minutes
- SO-INF01: Incident reporting timeliness (% reports delivered on time)

Minimum acceptable performance: Grade 3 (Satisfactory) per indicator.
Target performance: Grade 4 (Excellent) per indicator.
