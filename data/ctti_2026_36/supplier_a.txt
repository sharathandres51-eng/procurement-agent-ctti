technically capable and operationally mature response to this procurement.

=====================================================================
EQUIPMENT TECHNICAL OFFER
=====================================================================

The proposed CV-QKD system delivers a minimum net symmetric key generation rate of
1.5 kbps per emitter/receiver pair at 10 dB optical loss — exceeding the minimum
1 kbps requirement — with a security parameter of epsilon = 10^-10, an order of
magnitude tighter than the required 10^-9.

The KMS component is FIPS 140-3 certified and fully compliant with ETSI GS QKD 004
and ETSI GS QKD 014, including all key delivery methods. All equipment ships in
standard 19-inch rack format at 2U per main unit with a maximum depth of 550mm.

A key differentiator of our equipment offer is full DWDM-to-dark-fibre
reconfiguration capability via software-defined wavelength adjustment, requiring no
hardware replacement when network topology changes. This directly addresses the
flexibility requirement cited in Criterion 1.2 regarding adaptation to changes
between DWDM and dark fibre connectivity.

Resilience design includes dual redundant power supplies on all primary units and
automatic failover with a recovery time of 30 seconds. Critical optical components
are dual-sourced from two independent manufacturers, eliminating single-vendor
dependency at the component level. Each emitter and receiver can be replaced
independently; factory-paired units that require full system replacement are
explicitly absent from our offer.

=====================================================================
DEPLOYMENT RISK MANAGEMENT
=====================================================================

Our risk analysis for the deployment phase has identified the following scenario as
the primary technical risk warranting a specific mitigation plan:

Risk 1 — Optical signal degradation at legacy fibre nodes: Several nodes in the
target topology may present aged or non-characterised fibre runs. Degraded
physical layer performance at these nodes could compress the quantum channel margin
below the operational threshold. Our mitigation is a structured pre-installation
OTDR survey at each node, completed a minimum of three weeks before equipment
delivery, with a defined acceptance criterion for fibre qualification. Nodes failing
qualification will trigger a corrective fibre assessment before any QKD equipment
is commissioned at that site.

MONITORING AND SERVICE VISIBILITY
=====================================================================

A fully operational NOC dashboard will be live from day one of service, not at a
later milestone. The dashboard presents Secure Key Rate, excess noise, optical

including SKR thresholds, tampering alerts and hardware health indicators. The
platform integrates natively with CTTI SNMP and HTTP management infrastructure.
Alerting is automated with configurable threshold-based notifications routed to
both the QuantumNet NOC and CTTI monitoring teams simultaneously.
