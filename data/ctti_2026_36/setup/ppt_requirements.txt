SECTION 3.1 — QKD EQUIPMENT TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS (CV-QKD i QRNG)
=============================================================

3.1.1 Compatibility and Integration

The system must be based on CV-QKD with Prepare-and-Measure scheme, implementing
Gaussian Modulation of Coherent States (GMCS) protocols.

QKD equipment must meet the following requirements:

Optical compatibility:
- Equipment must guarantee coexistence of the quantum channel with standard C-band
  data (1530-1565 nm), ensuring interoperability and quality of service in mixed scenarios.
- Support for joint transmission of quantum and classical channels over a single optical
  fibre, using WDM multiplexing techniques without significant performance degradation.

Interoperability with network infrastructures:
- Compatibility with DWDM and CWDM systems, specifying exact compatible wavelengths.
- Capacity to customise the wavelength of the quantum channel.
- Equipment must allow co-propagation with at least 4 DWDM service channels without
  significant degradation of QKD system performance.
- Compliance with standards ETSI GS QKD 004 and 014, as well as ITU-T Y.3800.
- Support for standard management interfaces: SNMP and HTTP.
- Capacity for operation in point-to-multipoint (P2MP) scenarios.

Flexibility and maintenance:
- Possibility of independently replacing any device (emitter or receiver) without
  requiring replacement of the entire pair.
- Systems linked by fixed factory pairs that force complete system renewal in case
  of partial failure will NOT be accepted.

Integration with key management systems (KMS):
- Direct or indirect connection with KMS via compatible APIs (REST, JSON-RPC, KMIP).
- Support for key delivery protocols compatible with ETSI GS QKD 004 and 014.

3.1.2 Performance and Functionality

Minimum performance requirements:
- Maximum tolerated optical loss: minimum 10 dB.
- Power budget: maximum -10 dBm (10mW).
- Minimum net symmetric key generation rate:
  * >= 1 kbps per emitter/receiver pair under standard link conditions (10 dB)
  * >= 25 bps per service with KMS request every 10 seconds
  * >= 500 bps in multi-user scenarios and maximum expected network conditions (16 dB)
- The system must achieve at minimum 7,000 AES keys/hour @ 10 dB, and up to
  10,000 AES keys/hour @ 16 dB under optimal conditions.

Reliability, autonomy and operational security:
- The system must operate autonomously and continuously without critical external dependencies.
- Allow configuration as primary or secondary (redundant) entropy source.
- Have contingency mechanisms in case of quantum source failure (flow blocking, alerts);
  automatic generation of synthetic data is PROHIBITED.

Operability and management:
- Support for automatic key regeneration and automatic recovery from errors or interruptions.
- Remote and secure firmware update.
- Complete technical documentation and operational manuals in Catalan must be delivered.

3.1.3 Physical Format and Installation
- 19" rack format, maximum depth 600mm, maximum height 2U per main unit.
- 1U additional permitted for justified auxiliary equipment (power, supervision, etc.)
- Compatible with installation in standard telecommunications cabinets.

3.1.5 Management, Control and Monitoring
- Remote control and secure management via: SSH, SNMPv3, REST API, or Netconf/YANG.
- Real-time monitoring of critical parameters: optical losses, noise, received signal power.
- Specific QKD values monitoring including Secure Key Rate (SKR) and Excess Noise.
- Complete activity logs with audit export capability.
- Continuous log export to CTTI and Agència de Ciberseguretat collection platforms.
- Alert system for hardware performance and status: CPU consumption, key distribution
  latency, temperature and humidity.
- Integration with CTTI monitoring and observability tools.

3.1.6 Security
- Protection against known side-channel attacks including local oscillator manipulations.
- Generated keys must be random and unconditionally secure per internationally recognised
  security models.
- Automatic failure or tampering detection mechanisms (self-check, watchdogs).
- The QKD system must guarantee security parameter epsilon <= 10^-9.

3.1.7 Certification and Standards
- Certification or third-party validation based on: NIST SP 800-90B, DieHarder,
  TestU01 or equivalents.
- Preferably Common Criteria certification or accreditation by official agencies
  such as ANSSI, NIST, CCN or similar.

SECTION 3.2 — KMS EQUIPMENT TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================

3.2.1 Compatibility and Protocols
- Must be fully compatible with QKD systems based on quantum distribution protocols
  including CV-QKD (Continuous Variable) and DV-QKD (future hybrid compatibility).
- Must incorporate: Quantum-safe Key Relay between nodes implementing ETSI GS QKD 015.
- Internal entropy source based on QRNG with fallback generation mechanisms.
- Compliance with FIPS 140-2 or FIPS 140-3, or Common Criteria EAL2+ for internal
  cryptographic modules (HSM).
- Redundant key routing and secure storage.
- High availability (HA) at QKD node level.
- Integration via standard interfaces: ETSI GS QKD 014 (all methods including SKIP),
  ETSI GS QKD 004, QKD Network Layer (QNL) for multipoint topology.
- Compliance with PKCS#11, compatibility with HSM architectures.
- Must be based on Unix or similar operating system.
- Must support minimum 5 concurrent QKD modules and 5 KMS instances simultaneously.

3.2.2 Key Management Capabilities
- Capacity to distribute QKD-generated keys to multiple recipients securely,
  scalably and traceably.
- Support for unicast, multicast and broadcast keys.
- Support for key per service, per session and per device.
- Capacity to operate in P2P mode and star, hub-and-spoke or mesh topology.
- Integration with identity and access control systems: LDAP, RADIUS, SAML, OAuth2/OpenID.
- Role-based access control (RBAC) policy definition.
- Mutual authentication support between entities (MFA, digital certificates).
- KMS interfaces must be protected via HTTPS over TLS 1.3 with mutual certificate
  authentication per ETSI GS QKD 014.
- Automatic backup and secure restoration with encryption of keys at rest and in transit.
- Complete key lifecycle management: generation, distribution, use, rotation, revocation
  and destruction.

3.2.3 Security and Supervision
- Protection against physical unauthorised access (anti-tampering).
- Protection against logical attacks (DoS, side-channel, command injection).
- Integrity monitoring and verification mechanisms.
- Cryptographically signed logging support.
- Complete activity and access logs including SIEM integration capability.
- Real-time management interface monitoring: node status, key transactions, errors,
  security events, SKR, ESKR, keys stored, keys consumed.
- SNMP monitoring support.
- WebUI graphical administration interface accessible via browser.

4.1.1 Team Role Functions

- Responsable de compte (Account Manager): Maximum contract responsible for the
  contractor facing CTTI. Responsible for economic, contractual, organisational and
  service delivery strategy aspects. CANNOT be subcontracted.

- Cap de projecte (Project Manager): Responsible for planning, directing, supervising
  and coordinating all activities related to projects, services and associated supplies.
  Manages assigned resources, risk and change monitoring, coordination with other
  providers or involved third parties. Must ensure service success and correct execution.

- Arquitecte (Architect): Responsible for global network architecture design, integrating
  classical communication elements with quantum communication devices and protocols.
  Must ensure technical coherence, system compatibility and security throughout the design,
  taking into account QKD (CV-QKD) and KMS requirements. Technological reference for
  architecture and technology solutions.

- Administradors de xarxes i comunicació quàntica (Network and Quantum Administrators):
  Responsible for all administration tasks, procedures and review of the network including
  physical, virtual or cloud equipment.

- Operadors de xarxa i seguretat (Network and Security Operators): Technicians responsible
  for basic administration, operation and real-time monitoring of communications network
  infrastructure and associated security services.

- Enginyer/a de seguretat i criptografia (Security and Cryptography Engineer): Responsible
  for ensuring the deployed infrastructure meets security requirements throughout the system
  lifecycle, particularly regarding information protection, QKD-KMS integration and
  interoperability with classical encryption systems.

- Coordinador/a d'integració i proves (Integration and Testing Coordinator): Responsible
  for designing, planning and supervising system tests including performance, interoperability,
  stability and security tests during deployment and validation phases.

- Serveis professionals de tercer nivell (Level 3 Professional Services): High-level
  technician specialised in supplied systems and equipment. Critical in production
  commissioning, resolution of maximum-complexity incidents and evolutionary maintenance.
  Minimum 100 hours during the contract.

SECTION 5 — SERVICE DELIVERY PHASES
=============================================================

5.1 Phase 1 — Pla de migració (Migration Plan)
Duration: 4 months from contract start date.

Must include:
- Concrete definition of objectives: node categorisation, location definition,
  prioritisation criteria, requirements analysis
- Topology description
- Prerequisites
- Final new architecture design
- Description of Phases 2 and 3 (critical execution and deployment):
  nodes involved, equipment per node, detailed planning with dates, rollback plans
- Testing and validation definition including integration tests per QKD and KMS node
- Risk assessment and mitigation measures
- Timeline and associated resources description
- Access to project monitoring tool updated at minimum daily
- Management of technical access to Generalitat nodes and locations

5.2 Phase 2 — Execució crítica (Critical Execution)
Duration: 16 months from Migration Plan validation by CTTI.

Supply and installation of equipment at 4 of the 12 planned nodes.
Minimum activities:
- Equipment supply for first nodes
- Initial equipment deployment at first two nodes, basic configuration
- Calibration and production commissioning
- Third node scaling and impact evaluation
- Scaling to remaining nodes completing the 4 nodes of this phase
- Integration of commissioned equipment monitoring with control centre monitoring
- Maintenance and warranty management of production equipment

5.3 Phase 3 — Desplegament (Deployment)
Duration: 13 months from Critical Execution certification.

Deployment and expansion to remaining 8 nodes.
Minimum activities:
- Equipment supply for remaining nodes
- Scaling to 4 additional nodes closing the metropolitan ring
- Final scaling to last 4 nodes and locations
- Maintenance of laboratory environment operational throughout contract duration
- Laboratory must remain as permanent CTTI infrastructure after contract end

5.4 Phase 4 — Anàlisi de les dades (Data Analysis)
Duration: 24 months from Critical Execution commissioning certification.

Minimum activities:
- Traffic data analysis between first two nodes
- Traffic data analysis across first four nodes
- Traffic data analysis within metropolitan ring
- Complete network analysis
- Delivery of structured document with observation data, analyses and conclusions
- Training and knowledge transfer per Section 5.5

5.5 Pla de devolució del servei (Service Handover Plan)
Execution period: 2 to 4 months before contract end.

Minimum requirements:
- Knowledge transfer methodology including at minimum 2 sessions of 8 hours each
  covering: equipment and services training, manufacturer procedures roadmap,
  user manuals, process documentation, knowledge base
- All training and documentation provided in Catalan as minimum
- Practical tutored training where CTTI staff perform tasks supervised by contractor
- Offer of hardware and equipment assigned exclusively to contract services to CTTI
  or designated third parties
- Equipment valuation by third party using market price criterion
- Handover must not cause any service discontinuity
- Complete updated service management documentation (knowledge base) must be
  available before handover phase begins
