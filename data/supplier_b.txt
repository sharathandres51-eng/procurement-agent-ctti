=====================================================================
EQUIPMENT TECHNICAL OFFER
=====================================================================

The proposed CV-QKD system meets the minimum performance thresholds specified in
Section 3.1.2 of the PPT: a net symmetric key generation rate of >= 1 kbps per
emitter/receiver pair at 10 dB optical loss. The KMS component is FIPS 140-2
certified and compliant with ETSI GS QKD 004 and 014. All units are supplied in
standard 19-inch rack format within the 2U height constraint. Power supply
redundancy is provided on all primary units. Emitter and receiver units can be
replaced independently without requiring full system renewal.

DEPLOYMENT RISK MANAGEMENT
=====================================================================

CyberQuantum has undertaken a thorough risk analysis of the CTTI deployment
scenario and has identified three high-probability, high-impact risks with specific,
actionable mitigation plans for each.

Risk 1 — Optical signal degradation at nodes with legacy fibre infrastructure:
Older fibre runs in the CTTI topology may present attenuation characteristics
or connector quality that compress the quantum channel operating margin. Probability:
high. Impact: service commissioning delay or reduced SKR at affected nodes.
Mitigation: a mandatory pre-installation OTDR survey and physical fibre
qualification will be conducted at every node prior to equipment delivery. A formal
qualification acceptance criterion is defined; nodes failing this criterion will
undergo fibre remediation before any QKD hardware is deployed at that location.

Risk 2 — KMS-QKD synchronisation latency under high concurrent load: Under
conditions of maximum concurrent key requests across multiple service consumers,
management plane traffic may compete with key distribution traffic for bandwidth,
introducing latency that degrades key availability to downstream applications.
Probability: medium-high during peak periods. Impact: service degradation for
latency-sensitive key consumers. Mitigation: a dedicated management VLAN will be
provisioned for all QKD and KMS control plane traffic, with QoS policies enforcing
strict priority queuing for key distribution flows over all other management traffic
on that VLAN.

Risk 3 — Firmware incompatibility between QKD emitter and KMS versions during
phased rollout: Where nodes are brought into service sequentially over the
deployment schedule, version mismatches between already-commissioned emitter
firmware and the KMS version running at the time of each new node activation
carry a risk of protocol negotiation failures or reduced functionality.
Probability: medium given a multi-node phased rollout. Impact: node activation
failures requiring unplanned engineer visits. Mitigation: a staged firmware
validation protocol will be executed at each node prior to production
commissioning, including a verified rollback procedure. A compatibility matrix
will be maintained and updated after every firmware change across the deployed
estate.

MONITORING AND SERVICE VISIBILITY
=====================================================================

CyberQuantum will deploy a comprehensive NOC monitoring platform covering the
full CTTI QKD estate. The platform tracks Secure Key Rate in real time with
automated alerting triggered at configurable SKR threshold breaches. Optical
loss, noise and received signal power metrics are continuously monitored per
node. The web-based dashboard is accessible to both CyberQuantum operations
staff and authorised CTTI personnel. Data is refreshed on a three-minute cycle,
exceeding the standard five-minute monitoring frequency. Native SNMP integration
is configured for the CTTI infrastructure collection platform from day one.
